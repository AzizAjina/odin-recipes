Omurice

Omurice is a food with cooked rice wrapped in an egg and topped with ketchup or other sauces.

It is a combination of the English word “omelet” (or the French word “omelette”) and “rice”

It is often made not only in Western style restaurants but also in general diners and homes in Japan.

Ingredients

  • Seasoned Rice
  • Egg
  • Sausage chicken
  • Onions
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Ketchup

Steps

  1. Cut the sausage into 5 mm thick and mince the onion.
  2. Heat a frying pan, add oil (not included), and saute onions and sausages over medium heat.
  3. Put the cooked rice in a bowl, add the sauteed onions and sausage, and mix well. Add ketchup, salt and pepper and repeat mixing well.
  4. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add salt and beat well.
  5. Heat a small frying pan (20cm-22cm) over medium heat (a non-stick pan is the best).
  6. Add butter and turn the pan around to melt it, then turn the heat to low and add the eggs.
  7. Stir the eggs as if you were making scrambled eggs. Then shake the pan to prevent the eggs from sticking, and turn off the heat when the eggs are half-cooked.
  8. Place seasoned rice in the middle of the egg, lengthwise, and use a wooden spatula or heat-resistant rubber spatula to cover the rice with the egg.
  9. Hold a plate in one hand, the pan in the other hand, and flip the pan and move the omurice to the plate.
  10. Shape it into spindle form (American football shape!!).
  11. Put some ketchup in the corner of a Ziploc (plastic bag), cut the very tip a little bit, and write letters with a ketchup coming out of that part.
  12. Sereve it!!!
